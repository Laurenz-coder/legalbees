Overview

The EU Whistleblower Directive was adopted to strengthen legal protections for whistleblowers across the European Union. It is designed to protect those who report breaches of EU law in a work-related context from retaliation. The directive covers a wide range of areas, including public procurement, financial services, money laundering, product and transport safety, nuclear safety, public health, consumer and data protection, and more.
